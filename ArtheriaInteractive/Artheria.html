Many mages have tried to categorize magic. But in the world of Artheria, with its constant experimentations and violence, the many mage scholars do not last long. A lot of the information is reduntant, the same topic explored in different terms written in different languages.

The Future of Magic

Maybe one day, magic will be common, and will be a luxury that all can afford. But with what scholars currently know, that is near impossible. A single gifted scholar, who can summon the force of hurricanes and meteors, exude mana pressure. While some machines can use this pressure, the machines would span miles upon miles, filling empty cities and towns to do what a single sentient person could do.r

The Balance of Magic

A sorcerer from long ago noticed that the natural cycle of magic is the same as the circle of life. It's a closed ecosystem where if enough magic is drawn to an area, another area loses it. The form it takes, is often unknown. The drawing of mana that destroys a forest could be used to bring lift to super fauna that grow to be meters tall. There is a law of conservation, as with all science.

Magic is for all

Some magic users are innate. Some are given the power by a diety. These innate magic users have a battery for magic. Those who generate their own mana separate from their natural body's mana, are sorcerers.

But magic can be learned by everyone. Wizards exist to teach how to harness the magic of everyday life.

Magic Equation

There is an equation to magic. A lot of it is lost, in the many libraries of insane magic users. Often they are kept in tomes.

The Great Reset

There was an event. spells no longer worked. As such, there is now a hunt for the lost books. The magic that was lost to this world.

The Lost Art of Prestidigitation

Before, magic users could do whatever they wanted with their spells. often , the same magic that can be used to summon a small water fountain from your fingertips can be used to summon a fire.

Such magic is now considered Lost Magic. Now, all spells are specific in their output. The cost of Lost Magic was that it was unstable. There was no way to know the effects it had until further down the line. In one case, a wizard a grew a 2nd brain in her eyes. She at first reported blurry vision, and thoughts that were not her own. the condition worsened, until her eyes burst, revealing soft matter underneath them.

as such, Lost Magic must be researched, broken down in a controlled environment.

Spell Customization

The beauty of advanced spellcraft is that when all the expenses are paid, a team of sorcerers can research, run tests, and produce a modern spell tome. The spell can be copied into a wizard's method, and cleanly reproduced without the ill effects of Lost Magic.

Exporting the Story

What to do with your story when it's done:

Take the story to as big an audience as you can find, who want to listen to it.
Dungeons and Dragons, for example. It started small but grew into a massive fanbase. People even made their own versions like Pathfinder, thanks to open game licensing. What's cool is that from its humble beginnings in the 1970s, D&D now inspires comics and movies.
Now, think about your own tabletop RPG. You can turn it into a book, a novel, a comic, or even act it out with your buddies for a fun YouTube video. The key here is creating your own world and story. Why is this important?
Because you have control. Your actions and choices shape the narrative, making it a truly unique piece of art.
Compare this to playing a video game where, no matter how heroic your deeds, once the game is over, it's just fanfiction. Your impact on someone else's world is limited. But when you create your own world with friends, everyone's contribution is essential. It's a collaborative process where each person's input matters, making the experience richer and more meaningful for everyone involved.
